RVCC SPOTLIGHT

Dianne Henderson
OTA Club President
Dianne Henderson

As president of the Student Occupational Therapy Assistant Association, Dianne is passionate about helping improve the quality of life for individuals with impairments. RVCC was the first NJ community college to offer the Occupational Therapy Assistant program.

Ricky Henry
CEM-Tech Student
Ricky Henry

Ricky is one of a growing number of students pursuing a degree or certificate in one of todays hottest fields commercial energy management. Graduates of the program are in high demand with a 100% job placement rate.

Hien Ngo & Kathleen Stavole
PTK National Honorees
Hien Ngo & Kathleen Stavole

Phi Theta Kappa chapter president Kathleen and vice president Hien received international and regional awards for their exceptional leadership of the Alpha Epsilon Pi chapter of the international honor society.

Sophie Rosenberg
Event Planner Extraordinaire
Sophie Rosenberg

As students in the Events Planning class, Sophie and her classmates planned the Nota Bene reception, an annual showcase of outstanding student work from across academic disciplines. This years theme was a Hollywood award show.

Khayla Flemmings
Winning Teammate
Khayla Flemmings

Khayla helped drive the womens basketball team to its second straight 22-win season. The Exercise Science major was a constant threat on the court. She recorded 16 three-point field goals on the year and ranked first on the team among all freshmen in three-pointers per game.

Sidney Boyd
Animal Ethics Advocate
Sidney Boyd

As founder and president of the Animal Ethics Club, Sidney raises awareness of animal welfare and encourages a compassionate, healthy lifestyle for all living beings. The Communications major has organized events on campus to promote plant-based cafeteria options.

Paul Hamilton
Baseball Standout
Paul Hamilton

As a freshman baseball team member, Paul has been hitting .320 with a team-leading 8 doubles and 6 home runs. The Exercise Science major was named to the Region XlX All-Academic team.

Alaysha Walker
Social Justice Award Recipient
Alaysha Walker

The Assistant Director of Student Life received the Barbara W. McConnell Social Justice Award from SAFE in Hunterdon. The annual award recognizes an individual who exemplifies selfless giving to support survivors and raises awareness of domestic violence.

Kaeley Ipson
Newman Civic Fellow
Kaeley Ipson

Kaeley was one of 262 college students nationwide recognized by Campus Compact for her leadership skills and investment in solving public problems. The Honors College student and Civil Engineering major served as Enactus president, vice president of Communications for the Society of Women Engineers, and was involved in community service projects.

Aiden Nodoro
Rotaract Leader
Aiden Nodoro

The Rotaract vice president traveled to Nepal to deliver school and personal supplies to schools and orphanages. He, along with 5 other Rotaract members, visited schools in remote areas and met with children who had lost parents in the recent earthquake.

Claudia Hawse
Communities of Tomorrow
Claudia Hawse

As president of the new student club, Claudia advocates for expanding services for under-resourced students on campus and in the community and helped organize the campus food drive. The Social Work major volunteers at the Colleges Resource Center as an AmeriCorps Vista member.

Cori Fonzetti & Helen Holton
Service Learning Honorees
Cori Fonzetti & Helen Holton

The Cosmetology students received the Colleges Service Learning Leadership Award for their year-long Cut it Out project to engage Cosmetology and Esthetics students in learning about domestic violence and how to combat it.
