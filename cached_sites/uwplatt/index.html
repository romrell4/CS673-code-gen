Opportunity from the Start

Founded in 1866, the University of Wisconsin-Platteville is a comprehensive, four-year public institution of higher education. At UW-Platteville, students will find challenging academics; specialized, renowned faculty; and notable alumni—in a supportive, safe setting that is perfect for learning.

Our world-class faculty conduct classes on three campuses—UW-Platteville, UW-Platteville Richland, and UW-Platteville Baraboo Sauk County—that serve the needs of the region. Together, we are leaders in science, technology, agriculture, and engineering fields, with unique initiatives in microsystems and nanotechnology, forensic investigation, and renewable energy. And, UW-Platteville is among the most affordable institutions in the state, so you’ll earn a degree in your chosen field at a lower cost.

As a UW-Platteville student, you’ll find opportunity for growth and satisfaction through research, internships, membership in professional clubs and student organizations, living learning communities, athletics, and so much more. 

Make UW-Platteville your first choice for an outstanding education that helps you build the future you want.

Career Fair

Plan to attend the Spring 2020 Career Fair at the Williams Fieldhouse on Monday, February 3 from noon to 5 p.m.

Expert Professors Help You Succeed

Dedicated faculty support your learning and innovation in the classroom.

Hands-on experience

At UW-Platteville, classroom knowledge is enhanced with hands-on projects, labs, fieldwork, service learning, capstone courses, and undergraduate research.

Three campuses, one mission

The campuses of UW-Platteville, UW-Platteville Baraboo Sauk County, and UW-Platteville Richland share a mission of providing affordable access to a quality education.

Transformative Facilities

A new, state-of-the-art 100,000-square engineering facility is set to open in 2022. Sesquicentennial Hall will feature a green roof, makerspaces, and many sustainable features.

Affordable Opportunities

UW-Platteville is excited to offer the Pioneer Pledge. Pioneer Pledge guarantees that the cost of undergraduate, on campus tuition and fees will be covered for new freshmen attending the Platteville campus who are Pell-eligible Wisconsin residents.
