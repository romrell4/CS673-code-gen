Select the topics to explore just a few of the many reasons for choosing community college.

Because of their unique relationship with local businesses, community colleges can offer special programs to students with unique opportunities and learning experiences. FDTC's partnership with Caterpillar and their ThinkBIG program is a great example of these partnerships. While enrolled in the program, students learn to work on full-size caterpillar equipment provided by caterpillar.

When compared to four year institutions, the cost to attend community colleges is MUCH more affordable. The average cost of in-state tuition for four year institutions in South Carolina is $16,189 per year. The average cost of in-state tuition for two year community colleges is $4,486 per year. How much money could you save by going to community college?

Community Colleges are creating tons of ways to obtain a degree that are convenient for you. At a community college like FDTC you have options to attend classes at multiple campuses, during the day or night, during the week or weekends, or entirely online. Whatever your situation, FDTC probably has an opportunity waiting for you.

We know that every student has their own challenges to learning something new. That's why community colleges like FDTC keep class sizes small - so you can get the personal attention and assistance that you need to complete your program at your pace.

FDTC

The Florence-Darlington Technical Education Center was established in 1963 and presently serves Florence, Darlington, and Marion counties. The College's initial enrollment of 250 students now exceeds 6,000 curriculum students. Its primary campus of less than 10 acres has expanded to nearly 240 acres with a modern complex of eight major buildings totaling nearly 350,000 square feet.

SiMT

Today, the Southeastern Institute of Manufacturing & Technology houses the most advanced additive manufacturing center and 3D virtual reality production studio in the region and the only commercially available direct-to-metal 3D printer in the Southeast. Working closely with FDTC, our staff engineers routinely help conceive products, manufacture precision parts, and produce virtual training experiences.

About Us

Since 1963, Florence-Darlington Technical College has been serving South Carolina's Pee Dee Region by providing a quality education to the community workforce.

The Florence-Darlington Technical Education Center was first established in 1963 with an initial enrollment of 250 students. FDTC now boasts an enrollment of more than 6,000 students and serves South Carolina's Pee Dee Region by providing a quality education to the community workforce.

In 2007, FDTC completed construction of the Southeastern Institute of Manufacturing and Technology (SiMT). This facility provides unique learning opportunities for FDTC students, promotes our relationship with the local business community, and reinforces our commitment to regional economic development.

Academics

With over 85 programs available including diplomas, certificates, and degrees, FDTC offers many different options for you to increase your earning potential. Our academic programs and job training are designed to provide knowledge & skills that are field-specific. This no-fluff approach to learning helps our graduates find employment in a quick and cost-effective way.

FDTC partners with businesses in the region to design academic programs that are directly applicable to real-world jobs. This means that our academic programs are catered to train you as quickly as possible to address real-world problems.
