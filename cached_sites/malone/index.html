Malone's

Pendle Hill Pledge

Malone University’s new Pendle Hill Pledge is a campus-wide focus on career development and personalized mentorship, which helps students discover who they are meant to be and how their careers can be a part of a purposeful and meaningful life. Beginning fall 2020, all traditional undergraduate students at Malone will be guided by faculty, staff, peers, and curriculum to individually discover their sense of purpose and vocation through mentoring, experiential learning, and career support across the curriculum.

Kristen Heffron Moore '04

Serving as a magistrate for Stark County

Practicing law was not the career field alumna Kristen Heffron Moore '04 thought she'd be pursuing after graduating from Malone with an English degree. Desiring to live a life of service, Moore used the skills she learned during her undergraduate study to embark on the journey of law school. Since graduating from law school, Moore has served the community in a variety of ways, but most recently has taken on the role of Magistrate for Stark County.

Jonathon Detweiler '12

Operating local organic farm focused on community

Graduating with a degree in Bible and Theology, Jonathon Detweiler '12, put his degree to use in a non-traditional way. Detweiler owns and operates Venture Heritage Farm in rural Dalton which provides a plethora of wholesome, healthy crops and a place for the community to gather and connect.

Love Your Skin mural in downtown Canton
