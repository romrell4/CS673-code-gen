St. John's By the Numbers

7:1

The low student-to-faculty ratio of 7:1 contributes to the No. 8 ranking for “Best Classroom Experience” by Princeton Review.

See more rankings and ratings

200+

Johnnies study more than 200 great books, texts, and works in what is often called the most rigorous curriculum in America.

The St. John’s College Reading List

$35k

St. John’s undergraduate tuition of $35,000 is one of the most affordable among top liberal arts colleges in the country.

Learn about the St. John’s Financial Model

2

The Graduate Institute offers two master’s degrees: one in Liberal Arts and one in the Eastern Classics of Asia.

Learn more about Graduate Institute

50%

Half of all Johnnies do at least one internship, many of which are fully-funded through the Hodson and Ariel internship programs.

#5

The Princeton Review ranks St. John’s as fifth best in the country for top professors. Of course, at St. John’s we call them “tutors.”

Check out our rankings

80%

Most Johnnies live on campus in Annapolis or Santa Fe, including all freshmen and sophomores.

7,000

Santa Fe sits at a rarified 7,000 feet with access to 478 miles of hiking and biking trails. 79 ski trails are 15 miles uphill from campus.

Join the outdoor adventure

30

The Annapolis campus is just 30 minutes from Washington, D.C., making it one of the best colleges for politically engaged students.

Read about subjects in politics

2

St. John’s has two campuses in two state capitals: Santa Fe, New Mexico and Annapolis, Maryland.

Visit our beautiful campuses

3-2

Johnnies hold the national collegiate croquet championship title and last year beat the US Naval Academy 3-2 at the world-famous Annapolis Cup.

Explore St. John’s Traditions

1696

Johnnies have been reading great books since 1696 (when we were called “King William School”), making St. John’s the third oldest college in the United States.

Learn more about our history

#1

Ranked as the college sending the highest number of students to PhD programs in the humanities, St. John’s sends students to pursue advanced degrees and directly into dozens of fascinating careers.

Read more about careers and grad school

Top 4%

St. John’s is in the top 4% for the number of students receiving PhDs in science and engineering. The curriculum includes four years of advanced mathematics, physics, chemistry, and biology.

Read more about the sciences at St. John’s

2+2

St. John’s has no majors, but if you add up the credits, the degree would include a double major in philosophy and the history of math and science, plus a double minor in literature and classics.

Learn more about the curriculum

News and Features

Stay up-to-date with the latest St. John’s news and get to know our students, alumni, tutors, and friends.

Treating the Mind

Annapolis alum Omar Manejwala, MD (A93) is an international expert and speaker in the fields of addiction and mental health.

Devendra Contractor (SF79) Talks Santa Fe, Art, and Architecture

Santa Fe alum Devendra Contractor (SF79) designed the New Mexico Museum of Art’s new Vladem Contemporary.

A Message of Solidarity

Annapolis alum Lydia Polgreen (A97) is the editor-in-chief of The Huffington Post.

Meet the Johnnies: Shannon Hateley (SF07)

Shannon Hateley (SF07) is a computational biologist and senior staff scientist at Ancestry.

Alum Explores Literary History and Garners Acclaim with 2019 Book

Annapolis alum Andrew Hui received stellar reviews from publications including The New Yorker and The Times Literary Supplement for his 2019 book, A Theory of the Aphorism: From Confucius to Twitter.

A Year to Remember for Meow Wolf

Graduate Institute alum Sean Di Ianni (SFGI13) is one of the founders of arts collective Meow Wolf.

Annapolis Alum Explores the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence

Annapolis alum Jamaal Sebastian-Barnes has pursued a career in technology, with one big question: how do we explore the ethics of artificial intelligence?

From Monk to Money Manager: One Alum's Journey

Santa Fe alum Douglas Lynam (SF96), a former monk, is a financial advisor and author.

Owners of Alumni-Run Software Company Share the Value of Johnnies in the Workplace

Johanna Wilson (A90) and Richard Smith (A90) are the co-founders of software company OpenPath Products.

Author Salvatore Scibona (SF97) on Novel Writing, Newton, and New Mexico

Santa Fe alum Salvatore Scibona (SF97) was a National Book Award finalist for his 2008 book, The End.

Johnnie Combines Great Books, Content Creation at the Children's Defense Fund

Jay Tram (A20) spent a summer Hodson internship at the Children's Defense Fund.

Learning, Language, and Culture at Middlebury

Thanks to a General Pathways Fellowship, Sebastian Huerta (SF20) spent a summer learning French at Middlebury Language Schools.

Recent Grad Lands Job in the Publishing World

Karlena Haase (A19) recently got a job at Bloomsbury Publishing, which she considers a direct result of her Hodson Internship experience this summer.

Meet the Johnnies: Nani Detti (SF20)

Nani Detti (SF20) received a Projects for Peace grant from the Davis Foundation and a Global Pathways Fellowship from St. John’s College.

St. John’s College Named “Four-Year College of the Year”

Leading education news publisher Education Dive named St. John’s the “Four-Year College of the Year” in the 2019 Dive Awards.

Class of 1986 Establishes Collegewide Scholarship Fund

Tia Pausic (A86) and Elisabeth Long (A86) were the “prime movers” for the establishment of the Class of 1986 Endowed Scholarship, which offers scholarships to students on both the Annapolis and Santa Fe campuses.
