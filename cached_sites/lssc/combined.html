LSSC campuses are now tobacco & smoke free

As part of the College’s commitment to promoting a safe, healthy, and comfortable environment for its students, visitors, and employees, LSSC is now tobacco & smoke free. No smoking, using tobacco products, or vaping of any substances is allowed indoors or outdoors on any LSSC property.

Programs

A.A. General Studies

The Associate in Arts degree is designed to prepare students with the courses needed for the freshman and sophomore levels of a bachelor’s degree. Upon completion, students have the choice of transferring to a four-year college or university. Through our DirectConnect to UCF program, LSSC graduates have guaranteed acceptance to the University of Central Florida. Continue reading »

A.A.S. Electrical Distribution Technology

Completion of the Electrical Distribution Technology (EDT) program offers a bright future to those interested in the high-demand field of line working. With just 60-credit hours, students can apply knowledge from the classroom to practical field situations. In the EDT program, students will master basic electrical theory, electrical distribution safe work places, and overhead pole line and underground electrical utility construction and maintenance. Continue reading »

A.S. Business Administration

The Business Administration A.S. degree serves a dual purpose—preparing students for a business program at a four-year college or university, and providing knowledge and skills necessary for employment. Whether starting in an entry-level position, or climbing the corporate ladder, our program provides the necessary tools to guide students in their chosen paths. Continue reading »

A.S. Computer Information Technology

An A.S. Computer Information Technology degree offers a variety of career choices, ranging from computer repair technicians to IT analysis. This program provides supplemental training for persons new to field, as well as persons previously or currently employed in these occupations. The program also prepares students for certification exams. Continue reading »

A.S. Criminal Justice Technology

The Criminal Justice A.S. degree program offers courses aligned with the challenging standards needed to work in this occupation. We offer training for those interested in working as criminal justice practitioners/supervisors/managers in law enforcement agencies, correctional institutions, juvenile courts, and more. The programs may also benefit professionals seeking salary incentive benefits or career enhancement. Continue reading »

A.S. Engineering Technology Substation

The Engineering Technology (ET) degree is a two-year program, preparing students for over 307,000 jobs in the high-demand technology field. Some specializations include substation/relay tech and protective relay and electronics. Our ET core classes can lead you directly into the workforce or to a number of Florida’s universities, state and community colleges. Continue reading »

A.S. Management Technology

The Industrial Management Technology A.S. degree covers topics including communication skills, leadership skills, human relations and employability skills, principles of management, personnel management, and general business procedures. Manager positions in architecture, public safety, and manufacturing are a few starting career options. This degree requires prior completion of a 900+ clock hour postsecondary certification to enroll. Continue reading »

A.S. Nursing

Graduates of this program are qualified to take the national licensure examination (NCLEX-RN) to practice as registered nurses. Students accepted in the limited-access program study subjects including sciences, nutrition, mental health and maternal-newborn nursing. Continue reading »

B.A.S. Organizational Management​​​​

The Organizational Management program focuses on developing leaders through training in self-awareness, conflict resolution, and positive supervision. Designed for the working adult, it is an evening program. Career entry and advancement opportunities may be found in public, private, and not-for-profit organizations. Continue reading »

B.S. Nursing

To enroll in the Nursing B.S. program, you must have completed an Associate of Science Degree in Nursing. The BSN offers opportunities for graduates who wish to start careers as Nurse Practitioners, Health Specialists, and Medical Public Health Social Workers. Continue reading »
