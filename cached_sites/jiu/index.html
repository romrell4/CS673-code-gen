Jones International University Has Closed

In March of 2015, Jones International University (JIU) announced to all students, faculty and staff that it would cease operations after graduating the Class of 2015. The closure comes after 22 years of dedicated service and JIU’s delivery of high-quality postsecondary education to thousands of students around the globe.

At this time, JIU is no longer accepting new students. Please review this page carefully as it contains important links and information for former JIU students.

Jones International University Accreditation

From 1999 through February 2016, Jones International University (“JIU”) was a fully online university headquartered in Centennial, Colorado that was regionally accredited by the Higher Learning Commission (“HLC” or the “Commission”). JIU was approved by the Commission to offer Associate’s, Bachelor’s, Master’s, Specialist and Doctoral degree programs in the fields of business and education.

On March 31, 2015, JIU announced that it was winding down its operations in accordance with a formal Teach-Out Plan approved by the Commission and filed with the U.S. Department of Education (the “USDOE”). JIU subsequently resigned accreditation from the HLC in February 2016.

JIU and Trident University International Announce Formal Transfer Agreement

JIU has entered into a formal transfer agreement with Trident University International that will provide all JIU students with individualized, custom learning plans and the opportunity to transition their studies to Trident University.

Trident University serves thousands of students and offers four doctoral programs with 12 concentrations, eight graduate programs with 42 concentrations and six undergraduate degrees with 29 concentrations. As part of the transition, customized, individual student plans have been created to make the move to Trident a seamless one.

Students interested in transferring to Trident, click here: https://www.trident.edu/jiu/welcome-jiu-students/

Trident University to Rename Its Business School After Jones International University Founder

JIU’s founder, Glenn R. Jones, passed away on July 7, 2015 at the age of 85. Mr. Jones was a pioneer in online and distance learning who devoted much of his life to his passionate belief that world-class education should be available to everyone, everywhere. In its pursuit of excellence, JIU became the first regionally accredited, fully online university ever.

In recognition of Mr. Jones’s military service, entrepreneurial spirit, and extensive contributions to the fields of education and business, Trident University International renamed its College of Business Administration “The Glenn R. Jones College of Business Administration” on July 24, 2015.

“We are proud to rename our college of business after Glenn R. Jones. Under his leadership, Jones International University has made a tremendous impact on the educational landscape – particularly in online and distance learning,”

– Travis Allen, President & CEO, Trident

Student Transcripts and Records

JIU has transferred all student transcripts and records to the Colorado Department of Higher Education. Please be aware that Jones International University and the Colorado Department of Higher Education are the only sources with authentic student records. Transcript and records requests can be made by following this link: https://www.parchment.com/u/registration/227403/account

Alternatively, students may contact the Colorado Department of Higher Education by phone at (303) 862-3001.
